About Me

ENGINEER AND BUILDER

I’m Shrey, an Engineering 1 student at McMaster University who thrives on building systems where mechanical design, electronics, and intelligent software come together. I gravitate toward problems that intersect robotics, automation, and data, where thoughtful engineering decisions can turn theory into systems that actually move, sense, and adapt in the real world.

Through my work with robotics teams and research labs, I’ve designed and tested mechanical components, supported experimental automation, and contributed to projects involving computer vision, control, and hardware–software integration. Most recently, I am conducting biomechanics research with a distinguished professor and McMaster University and am a Mechanical Team Member with the McMaster Mars Rover Team, where I am excited to continue developing robust mechanical systems and collaborating across disciplines to solve complex, real-world engineering challenges.

Outside of formal projects, I’m often experimenting with mechanical systems and electromechanical assemblies, tuning control logic, and analyzing sensor data to better understand how physical designs respond in real-world conditions. If it involves robotics, mechanisms, or closing the loop between the digital and physical worlds, I’m in.

McMaster University – Biomechanics Research Fellow

As a Biomechanics Research Fellow under Professor Ravi Selvaganapathy, I am designing and building a robotic arm system to automate radiopharmaceutical processing and testing. This involves integrating precision actuators, sensors, and custom hardware to improve the efficiency, accuracy, and repeatability of complex laboratory experiments. By creating a system capable of handling delicate procedures, I am helping reduce human error and streamline workflows in a highly regulated research environment.

I am developing and implementing Python pipelines to control the robotic system, collect experimental data, and perform real-time analysis for quality assurance. This includes creating automated routines for task execution, programming data logging processes, and implementing checks to ensure experimental consistency. Through these efforts, I am enabling the lab to generate reliable, high-quality data while supporting advanced experimental protocols that would be challenging to perform manually.

I am collaborating closely with a multidisciplinary team of graduate students and engineering faculty to plan experiments, troubleshoot technical issues, and optimize the robotic system’s performance. In addition, I contribute to producing detailed technical reports, data visualizations, and presentations, translating complex experimental results into actionable insights. This collaborative work enhances the lab’s ability to make data-driven decisions, improves the reproducibility of experiments, and supports ongoing innovation in robotic automation for biomedical research.

University of Waterloo – Alternative Fuels Research Fellow

Under the guidance of Professor Roydon Fraser, I supported graduate students and professors in the lab during alternative fuels experiments, assisting with tasks such as sample preparation, materials handling, and post-experiment processing. By helping manage these hands-on processes, I contributed to the smooth and efficient operation of complex experiments.

In addition, I meticulously recorded measurements and observations from each experiment, organizing the data for further analysis and ensuring that results were accurate, consistent, and accessible to the research team. This allowed the team to identify trends, compare outcomes, and make informed decisions for subsequent experimental designs.

University of Toronto - Autonomous Surgery Research Fellow

Under the guidance of Professor Leuder Kahrs, I collaborated with a team of graduate students to support the development and evaluation of robot-assisted surgical workflows, gaining hands-on exposure to academic research in medical robotics. Through this work, I contributed to designing experiments that improved the efficiency and reliability of surgical procedures assisted by robotic systems.

Using BoofCV, I applied medical image processing techniques to analyze surgical imagery, experimenting with feature detection, preprocessing methods, and data enhancement strategies. This work helped improve robotic perception and informed adjustments to the workflows, enabling more precise and consistent robotic actions during simulations.

In addition to software-focused tasks, I assisted with system testing, experimental setup, and data validation, ensuring that automation processes performed reliably across trials. By supporting both the technical and operational aspects of the research, I helped maintain high standards of experimental accuracy and contributed to actionable insights that advanced the team’s objectives in medical robotics.

teaBOT Inc. – Robotics and Engineering Intern

During my time working at teaBOT Inc., I disassembled and evaluated over 10+ water heaters, carefully documenting more than 15+ components and assessing their function to understand system performance. I tested heater placement and tubing configurations to ensure consistent water flow and reliable operation, identifying potential inefficiencies and areas for improvement. This hands-on work enhanced my practical understanding of mechanical systems, troubleshooting, and quality assurance in real-world applications.
